2.3. GWAS Model

logit(P[RPL=1]) = β0 + βSNP·dosage + βAGE·AGE + βBMI·BMI + βPC1·PC1 + βPC2·PC2 + βPC3·PC3 + βQ1·Q1 + βFROH·F_ROH

PLINK2 --glm uses Firth logistic regression (hybrid mode) by default when cell counts are small, which prevents separation issues with rare variants in small sample sizes.

3. Tier 1: PBS Candidate Association

3.1. Context & Hypothesis

The Population Branch Statistic (PBS, Yi et al. 2010) isolates allele-frequency divergence on the Uzbek branch using two outgroups (EUR and EAS). A high PBS signal at a locus means natural selection or strong drift has shifted allele frequencies in Uzbeks specifically — not in either outgroup. This makes PBS hits attractive candidates for traits where Uzbeks may carry population-specific risk modifiers.

Reproductive fitness is one of the most direct selection targets. If a variant has been driven to high (or low) frequency by selection in Uzbeks, and that variant happens to influence pregnancy maintenance, the effect on RPL risk could be detectable at modest sample sizes that would have no power for a generic genome-wide scan. Tier 1 therefore tests a small, biologically motivated hypothesis at a relaxed significance threshold, complementing the underpowered genome-wide scan in Tier 3.

3.2. Input

  • Variant set: 8 lead SNPs with PBS ≥ 0.3 from Step 10, post-LD-clumping (Step 13), distributed across chr 3, 5, 10, 11, 12.
  • Sample set: 273 cases × 509 controls (same as Tier 3).
  • Genotypes: Imputed dosages from UZB_imputed_HQ_clean (R2 ≥ 0.3, MAF ≥ 0.01).
  • Covariates: AGE, BMI, PC1–3 (variance-standardised).

3.3. Method

Variants are extracted from the BFILE by CHR:POS match against the BIM file (rsID is unreliable for low-frequency variants in imputed data). PLINK2 --glm Firth-hybrid logistic regression is run on the 8-SNP subset. Bonferroni correction for 8 independent tests gives the significance threshold p < 0.05/8 = 6.25×10−3.

3.5. Outcome

No PBS candidate reached the Bonferroni threshold (smallest p > 0.25; see live table above). All 8 OR confidence intervals span 1.0. This is a negative result for the selection ↔ reproductive-fitness hypothesis at this sample size.

3.6. Limitations

  • Low power for rare variants. Five of the eight have MAF < 0.05 in UZB, where minimum detectable OR at 80 % power and α=0.00625 is roughly 2.5–3.0.
  • PBS signal does not imply phenotypic effect. A locus can be highly differentiated because of pigmentation, immunity, metabolism, or pure drift — not necessarily reproduction.
  • Lead-SNP testing only. Causal variant may be in LD nearby; locus-based testing (e.g., SKAT on the LD block) is recommended as a follow-up but requires more samples.
  • 1000G outgroup choice. PBS used EUR + EAS as outgroups; replacing EAS with SAS (Uzbeks' nearest 1000G neighbour, FST=0.018) would shift the candidate set. See Step 14.

4. Tier 2: Known RPL Variant Screen

4.1. Context & Hypothesis

Four well-characterised thrombophilia / one-carbon-metabolism variants are repeatedly associated with recurrent miscarriage in European meta-analyses (Practice Committee ASRM, 2012; Sergi et al. 2014). Their allele frequencies in Central Asian populations are poorly characterised — most published frequencies are extrapolated from European, South Asian, or East Asian reference panels that may not capture Uzbek-specific drift.

Tier 2 has two purposes: (a) descriptive — provide UZB allele frequencies that can stand alone as a reference contribution, and (b) confirmatory — test whether the European-derived ORs replicate in this cohort.

4.2. Input

  • Variants: rs6025 (F5 Leiden), rs1799963 (F2 G20210A), rs1801133 (MTHFR C677T), rs1799889 (PAI-1 4G/5G).
  • Look-up strategy: rsID match against the BIM file. Imputation panels with only CHR:POS:REF:ALT IDs require an additional rsID→position cross-walk.
  • Sample set & covariates: identical to Tier 1 / Tier 3.

4.3. Method

Per-variant Firth logistic regression in PLINK2. Allele frequencies reported alongside OR so that even non-significant rows constitute a publishable frequency benchmark for Central Asia.

4.4. Variant Results

4.5. Outcome

Three of four variants tested: F5 Leiden p=0.87, F2 G20210A p=0.66, MTHFR C677T p=0.66 (none significant). PAI-1 4G/5G is an indel and was filtered out of the imputed dataset (not in HQ_clean BIM). UZB allele frequencies are 5–15 % lower than EUR for thrombophilia variants — consistent with the lower published RPL prevalence in Central Asia.

4.6. Limitations

  • Underpowered to confirm modest effects. Published European ORs for these variants (1.2–3.5) require >1000 cases for 80 % power at α=0.0125 (Bonferroni for 4 tests). Non-replication is therefore not evidence of absence.
  • PAI-1 4G/5G missing. Most imputation panels exclude the indel; alternative is direct genotyping (e.g., TaqMan).
  • RPL aetiology beyond thrombophilia. Anti-phospholipid syndrome, uterine anomalies, karyotypic abnormalities, and endocrine factors are not captured here.

5. Tier 3: Genome-Wide Scan

5.0. Context & Hypothesis

Tier 3 is the agnostic, hypothesis-free scan: every well-imputed common variant is tested for association with RPL using the same logistic-regression model as Tier 1/2. The aim is not to declare a genome-wide significant locus — with 273 cases that is unrealistic — but to (a) provide a transparent λGC calibration check that validates the model, (b) detect suggestive signals (p < 1×10−5) that can be carried forward to replication cohorts, and (c) make a Manhattan / QQ plot freely available for meta-analysis.

5.1. PLINK2 Command

plink2 --bfile UZB_imputed_HQ_clean \ --pheno gwas_pheno.txt --pheno-name RPL --1 \ --covar gwas_covar.txt \ --covar-name AGE,BMI,PC1,PC2,PC3 \ --covar-variance-standardize \ --glm hide-covar cols=chrom,pos,ref,alt,a1freq,test,nobs,orbeta,se,ci,tz,p \ --ci 0.95 \ --out tier3_gwas \ --threads 16 --memory 200000

7. Methodology Notes

7.1. Why Logistic Regression (not Mixed Models)?

For small sample sizes with modest relatedness, PLINK2 --glm with Firth regression is appropriate. BOLT-LMM / SAIGE are recommended for:

  • N > 5,000 (mixed models lose power below this)
  • Heavy cryptic relatedness (our PI_HAT > 0.0884: only 428 of 547,581 pairs = 0.08%)
  • Unbalanced case:control ratios > 1:10

Our ratio is 273:509 (1:1.86) — well balanced. PCs 1–3 adequately control for population structure in this dataset. Q1 (ADMIXTURE K=2) was dropped due to collinearity with PC1 (VIF too high). λGC = 0.978 confirms no inflation.

7.2. Sensitivity Analyses (Executed — see Section 8)

  1. S1 Strict ESHRE: ≥3 strict losses as CASE (97 cases × 509 controls). Tests the historical RCOG/ASRM-strict definition.
  2. S2 Include REVIEW: Reclassify 64 REVIEW (loss at GA≥20) as CASE (337 × 509). Tests robustness to outcome ascertainment/coding.
  3. S3 Include GRAY: Sporadic-loss women added to cases (392 × 509). Broadest definition; maximises power but blurs phenotype.
  4. S4 Include PROBABLE: 18 missing-GA cases + 3 probable controls reclassified by clinician judgement (291 × 512). Tests sensitivity to ambiguous/missing fields.

The objective was to test whether the primary suggestive signal at the chr18:70.49 Mb cluster (lncRNA region; nearest protein-coding SOCS6) and the absence of other genome-wide-significant hits are robust to plausible alternative phenotype encodings.

8.4. Interpretation

What this tells us:

  • The primary chr18:70.49M cluster signal (near SOCS6) is robust to phenotype perturbation — it appears in the primary analysis (273 cases, p=3.2e-7) and is reproduced in S4 (291 cases including PROBABLE re-classifications, p=2.9e-7) with identical direction (protective, OR≈0.35) and lead SNPs. It does not replicate in broader phenotypes (S2, S3) which suggests the protective effect is specific to the strict-RPL phenotype and is diluted by sporadic-loss cases.
  • S2/S3 surface a chr1:109.15Mb cluster (CELSR2/PSRC1/SORT1) with consistent direction (risk, OR≈2.0). This region is well-known for cardiovascular and lipid traits but has not been reported for RPL. Interpretation should be cautious: it appears only when broader case definitions are used, suggesting it may track a non-RPL-specific phenotype (vascular? metabolic?) that becomes more prevalent in broader case sets.
  • S3 surfaces a chr9:115.9Mb cluster (LINC00474; near PAPPA) (rs28446251 OR=2.12, p=1.0e-7; rs3037402 OR=1.95, p=8.1e-7). This signal also appears in S2 (p=6.2e-7) and at p=4.6×10−7 in the primary analysis (rank #14). Critically, rs3037402 was independently reported as a genome-wide-significant RPL hit by a concurrent group at EMGM 2026 (OR=2.50, p=3.8×10−8), described by them as “near PAPPA.” Ensembl-verified GRCh38 coordinates: rs3037402 (chr9:115,896,897) sits inside an intron of the long non-coding RNA LINC00474 (chr9:115,839,214–115,925,720); the nearest protein-coding gene is PAPPA (pappalysin 1, chr9:116,153,791–116,402,321), located ~257 kb downstream. PAPPA cleaves IGFBP-4/-5 to release IGF-1/-2 for trophoblast invasion, which is a plausible biology for early pregnancy maintenance. The convergence of two independent studies on rs3037402 substantially elevates the biological credibility of this locus. Recommended action: report this SNP explicitly in the RPL manuscript; pursue eQTL / colocalisation analysis for both PAPPA and LINC00474; and contact the EMGM group for fixed-effects meta-analysis. See full report: cross_study_report.html.
  • None of the four sensitivity runs detected a genome-wide-significant (p<5e-8) variant. This is consistent with the power calculation: at our cohort sizes the minimum detectable OR at the GW threshold is ≥3 even at common-variant MAF.
  • Lambda values are well-controlled across all runs (0.86–1.02). S1's deflation (0.86) is expected from Firth correction with only 97 cases and should not be over-interpreted.

Caveats: The chr1 and chr9 clusters appearing only in broader case definitions (S2/S3) are statistically expected to inflate when phenotype heterogeneity rises — they may reflect phenotypes other than strict RPL (e.g., vascular or metabolic risk that increases sporadic-loss frequency). These signals should not be reported as RPL-specific findings without independent replication in a strict-RPL cohort.
