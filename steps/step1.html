1 Overview

Per-sample missingness (F_MISS) quantifies the fraction of genotype calls that failed for a given individual. Elevated missingness indicates technical failures during genotyping — degraded DNA, low input concentration, partial hybridisation on the array, or scanning artefacts. Retaining such samples introduces differential missingness bias: variants appear to differ in frequency between high-quality and low-quality samples even when no true genetic difference exists, producing spurious associations and inflated test statistics.

Rationale: Sample-level missingness filtering is the first PLINK QC step because it prevents high-missingness samples from influencing SNP-level statistics computed later (HWE p-values, allele frequency estimates, per-SNP call rates). A relaxed 20% threshold was chosen to remove only catastrophic failures while retaining as much of the Central Asian cohort as possible. See Section 2 for threshold comparison with the literature.

Input: ConvSK_raw — the PLINK binary dataset exported from GenomeStudio (see Step Pre). 654,027 variants × 1,247 samples. Server: Biotech2024 · Path: /staging/ALSU-analysis/winter2025/PLINK_301125_0312/

2 QC Parameter: Per-Sample Missingness

PLINK computes per-sample missingness as F_MISS = N_MISS / N_GENO, where N_MISS is the number of failed genotype calls and N_GENO is the total attempted calls. The --mind flag removes all individuals with F_MISS above the threshold.

Note on N_GENO: PLINK internally excludes a small number of probes from the missingness calculation (multiallelic sites, duplicate positions, I/D alleles). The effective N_GENO in ConvSK_raw_miss.imiss is 620,492 — not 654,027. All F_MISS values in this page are computed against this denominator and are consistent with the removal threshold.

ParameterValue usedTypical rangeBiological interpretation
--mind0.20 (20%)0.01–0.10 Samples with >20% missing genotypes removed. Corresponds to failing >124,000 of 620,492 attempted genotype calls.

4 Cross-Reference with Step 0 Investigation

The Step 0 chip-level investigation independently categorised all 1,247 samples by examining chip barcodes, raw scan metrics, heterozygosity, and contamination indicators. Every one of the 99 samples removed by the F_MISS > 0.20 filter had already been flagged — a 100% overlap confirming that the threshold correctly captures all major technical failures.

ℹ
Why Step 0 shows 98 chip_failures but Step 1 shows 84. Step 0's removal breakdown covers all 191 total removals across every category. The 4 failed chips each hold 24 physical IDAT positions = 96 total positions. However, not all 96 positions appear as distinct non-d/t entries in the FAM file: two entries (e.g. 08-825 and 08-825d, both on a failed chip) are counted separately in Step 0 — one as chip_failure, the other as dt_artifact — giving 98 chip_failure + related d/t entries rather than a flat 96. In Step 1, which applies only the F_MISS > 0.20 filter, 84 of those positions actually exceed the threshold and appear in remove_miss20.txt; the remaining bad-chip samples are removed at Step 2 (IBD deduplication) because they are d/t replicate entries or because they were caught under a different primary removal criterion. The bottom line: no sample on a catastrophically failed chip is retained past Step 2.

      --missing \

# Produces:

# N_GENO in .imiss = 620,492 (PLINK excludes multiallelic / duplicate / I/D probes)

# Column 6 of .imiss = F_MISS
awk 'NR>1 && $6+0 > 0.20 {print $1"\t"$2}' \
