Step 3: SNP Quality Control & VCF Export

Filter variants by allele frequency, Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium, and genotyping rate; export to VCF for imputation

✓ Completed — December 17, 2025

1. Overview

After removing low-quality samples (Steps 1–2), this step applies variant-level quality control. Three filters are applied simultaneously: minimum minor allele frequency (MAF), Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium (HWE) test, and per-SNP genotyping rate. These filters remove uninformative, technically artefactual, or poorly genotyped variants before imputation.

Accurate imputation depends critically on having a clean set of typed variants. Low-quality or artefactual typed SNPs propagate errors into imputed genotypes — a phenomenon known as garbage in, garbage out. Pre-imputation QC is therefore more stringent than general analysis QC.

Why filter before imputation? Imputation servers (Michigan, TOPMed) use typed variants as the scaffold for inferring untyped variants. If a typed SNP has incorrect allele frequencies (due to genotyping error or population stratification artefact), all imputed variants in its LD neighbourhood inherit that error. Clean typed variants = clean imputed genome.

2. QC Parameters Explained

2a. Minor Allele Frequency (MAF)

MAF is the frequency of the less common allele at a biallelic locus. For a SNP with alleles A and G in N diploid individuals, if A appears 30 times and G appears 2,166 times, then MAF = 30 / (30 + 2,166) = 0.0137. Variants with very low MAF (<1%) are problematic for several reasons:

  • Statistical power: Extremely rare variants provide little discriminatory power in frequency-based analyses (PCA, Fst, association tests).
  • Genotyping error: At very low MAF, even a single miscall can dramatically shift the observed frequency, creating false rare-variant signals.
  • Imputation quality: Imputation servers have limited accuracy for variants with MAF < 1% in the typed panel.
ParameterValueEffect
--maf 0.01 MAF ≥ 1% Removes monomorphic and rare variants. The minor allele must appear in at least ~22 of 2,196 allele copies (1,098 individuals × 2).

2b. Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium (HWE)

The Hardy-Weinberg principle states that genotype frequencies in a large, randomly mating population remain constant across generations: if p is the frequency of allele A and q = 1 − p is the frequency of allele B, then genotype frequencies should be AA = p², AB = 2pq, BB = q². Departure from HWE at a given locus can indicate:

  • Genotyping error (most common cause in array data) — e.g., miscalling heterozygotes as homozygotes due to poor cluster separation.
  • Population stratification — different subpopulations have different allele frequencies, and mixing them violates the random-mating assumption.
  • Copy number variation (CNV) — duplicated regions produce excess heterozygosity.
  • Selection — genuine biological departure, but rare for individual loci.

For QC purposes, extreme HWE violation (p < 10⁻⁶) almost always indicates a technical problem rather than biology. Using a lenient threshold avoids discarding loci under genuine selection.

ParameterValueEffect
--hwe 1e-6 HWE p > 10⁻⁶ Removes only severely violating loci. A p-value of 10⁻⁶ indicates ~1 in a million chance the observed genotype frequencies arose under HWE — strongly suggesting a technical problem.

2c. Per-SNP Genotyping Rate

Per-SNP missingness is the fraction of individuals for whom a given variant could not be called. High SNP-level missingness indicates a probe-specific technical failure (e.g., poor hybridisation, non-specific binding, or a probe overlapping a common deletion). Such variants produce unreliable allele frequency estimates and should be excluded.

The --geno threshold of 0.01 requires ≥99% genotyping rate per SNP. This is stringent because imputation accuracy depends on reliable typed variants.

ParameterValueEffect
--geno 0.01 ≥ 99% call rate per SNP Removes SNPs missing in >1% of samples (>11 of 1,098 individuals).

2d. Autosome Restriction

Only autosomes (chromosomes 1–22) are retained for imputation. Sex chromosomes (X, Y) and mitochondrial variants require special handling (hemizygosity, different ploidy) and are excluded from the standard imputation pipeline.

ParameterValueEffect
--chr 1-22 Autosomes only Excludes chrX, chrY, chrMT, and any unplaced contigs

5. Commands Executed

Step 1: Apply combined SNP QC filters

$ plink --bfile ConvSK_mind20_dedup \ --maf 0.01 \ --hwe 1e-6 \ --geno 0.01 \ --chr 1-22 \ --make-bed \ --out ConvSK_mind20_dedup_snpqc # Result: 472,463 variants remain (181,564 removed) # 1,098 samples unchanged

Step 2: Convert to VCF format

# plink2 for VCF export (supports modern VCF spec) $ plink2 --bfile ConvSK_mind20_dedup_snpqc \ --export vcf \ --out ConvSK_mind20_dedup_snpqc PLINK v2.00a2.3 64-bit (17 May 2019) 472,463 variants and 1,098 samples pass filters.

Step 3: Split VCF by chromosome for imputation server

$ for chr in {1..22}; do bcftools view ConvSK_mind20_dedup_snpqc.vcf \ --regions $chr \ -O z -o michigan_ready_chr/chr${chr}.dose.vcf.gz bcftools index michigan_ready_chr/chr${chr}.dose.vcf.gz done # 22 chromosome VCF files ready for Michigan Imputation Server
