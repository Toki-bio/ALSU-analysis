Step 2: IBD Deduplication & Duplicate Removal

Identify and remove duplicate or related samples using Identity-by-Descent analysis

✓ Completed — December 16, 2025

1. Overview

This step identifies and removes genetic duplicates or close relatives from the dataset. Using Identity-by-Descent (IBD) analysis, samples sharing too much genetic material are detected and one copy from each pair/group is removed.

Rationale: Duplicate samples (technical replicates or identical twins) and close relatives violate the independence assumption in population analyses. Removing these relationships ensures valid statistical inferences in downstream analyses like GWAS.

4. Commands Executed

Step 1: Prune for LD

plink --bfile ConvSK_mind20 --indep-pairwise 50 5 0.2 --out ConvSK_mind20_pruned

Step 2: Calculate genome-wide IBD

plink --bfile ConvSK_mind20 --extract ConvSK_mind20_pruned.prune.in --genome --out ConvSK_mind20_ibd

Step 3: Extract duplicate pairs

awk 'NR>1 && $10 >= 0.98 {print $1, $2, $3, $4, $10}' ConvSK_mind20_ibd.genome > duplicates_pihat098.txt

Step 4: Generate removal list

awk '{print $3, $4}' duplicates_pihat098.txt > remove_dups_pihat098.txt

Step 5: Remove duplicates

plink --bfile ConvSK_mind20 --remove remove_dups_pihat098.txt --make-bed --out ConvSK_mind20_dedup
