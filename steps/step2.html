Step 2: IBD-Based Duplicate Removal

Identify and remove duplicate or near-identical samples using genome-wide identity-by-descent

✓ Completed — December 15, 2025

1. Overview

Identity-by-descent (IBD) analysis estimates the proportion of the genome shared between every pair of individuals. The statistic PI_HAT (also written π̂) summarises this: PI_HAT = P(IBD=2) + 0.5 × P(IBD=1). For unrelated individuals, PI_HAT ≈ 0; for parent–offspring or full siblings, PI_HAT ≈ 0.50; for monozygotic twins or duplicate samples, PI_HAT ≈ 1.0.

Duplicate or near-identical samples (PI_HAT ≥ 0.98) violate the independence assumption required by virtually all downstream statistical tests. Retaining them inflates test statistics, biases allele frequency estimates, and produces artefactual signals in PCA and ADMIXTURE analyses. The high PI_HAT threshold of 0.98 specifically targets technical duplicates (same DNA run twice) rather than biological relatives.

Rationale: A PI_HAT threshold of ≥ 0.98 identifies samples that are near-genetically-identical — either the same individual genotyped twice, aliquots from the same DNA extraction, or monozygotic twins. This is well above the expected PI_HAT for first-degree relatives (~0.50), ensuring that biological relatives are retained at this stage. Relatedness at lower thresholds is assessed separately in Step 15 (ROH & IBD).

2. QC Parameter: PI_HAT & Kinship Categories

PLINK's --genome command computes pairwise IBD estimates for all sample pairs. The key outputs are Z0, Z1, Z2 (probabilities of sharing 0, 1, or 2 alleles IBD) and PI_HAT = Z2 + 0.5 × Z1.

To avoid LD-induced inflation of IBD estimates, the calculation is performed on a pruned subset of SNPs (LD r² < 0.10, 44,782 independent markers).

5. Commands Executed

Step 1: LD-prune for IBD calculation

# Prune to independent SNPs (r² < 0.10) for unbiased IBD estimation $ plink --bfile ConvSK_mind20 \ --indep-pairwise 50 5 0.1 \ --out ConvSK_mind20_ibd_prune # Result: 44,782 independent SNPs retained in .prune.in

Step 2: Compute genome-wide pairwise IBD

$ plink --bfile ConvSK_mind20 \ --extract ConvSK_mind20_ibd_prune.prune.in \ --genome \ --out ConvSK_mind20_ibd # Output: ConvSK_mind20_ibd.genome # Contains PI_HAT for all 657,778 sample pairs

Step 3: Extract duplicate pairs (PI_HAT ≥ 0.98)

# Column 10 = PI_HAT $ awk 'NR>1 && $10 >= 0.98 {print $1, $2, $3, $4, $10}' \ ConvSK_mind20_ibd.genome > duplicates_pihat098.txt $ wc -l duplicates_pihat098.txt 65 duplicates_pihat098.txt

Step 4: Cluster-based deduplication

# Build relatedness graph from PI_HAT >= 0.98 edges # Find connected components = duplicate clusters # Keep best sample per cluster (lowest F_MISS from ConvSK_mind20_miss.imiss) # 65 pairs → 106 unique nodes → 49 clusters # Kept: 49 (1 per cluster) | Removed: 57 # Output: remove_dups_pihat098.txt (FID IID) # dup_clusters_summary.tsv (cluster_id, kept, size, members)
